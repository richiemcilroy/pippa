# First User And Jobs To Be Done

Source section: 3. Founder clarification: Girls in Marketing is behind-the-scenes marketing strategy, not necessarily a visible in-product launch community.

## First User

The first user is a mid-20s professional woman.

Whitepaper description:

- Digitally native.
- Follows wellness and nutrition content.
- Familiar with protein, fibre, steps, workouts, and cycle tracking.
- Does not feel truly in control of her body or habits.
- Wants to lose weight and is comfortable admitting that.
- May have lost weight before, but previous attempts have not lasted.

## Core Problem

Her issue is not ignorance. It is consistency under real-life conditions.

Specific consistency pressures named in the whitepaper:

- Work stress.
- Social meals.
- Weekends.
- PMS cravings.
- Bloating.
- Cycle-related hunger.
- Imperfect logging.
- Discouragement after one failed day.

## Current App Problem

Existing apps often give her numbers but not context. They also make tracking feel private, clinical, and visually unshareable, even though her wider life is social and content-native.

## Beachhead Distribution

Liv's Girls in Marketing audience creates a specific launch wedge:

- Ambitious, digitally native women.
- Already share professional, lifestyle, and wellness content.
- The app can meet them where they are: aesthetic, social, accountable, and practical.

Founder clarification: this should be treated as marketing strategy behind the scenes, not as a default visible in-product launch community.

## Jobs To Be Done

| Job type | Job |
| --- | --- |
| Primary job | Lose weight sustainably without feeling like she is fighting her body. |
| Emotional job | Feel understood, less guilty, and more in control. |
| Behavioural job | Track consistently enough to create a calorie deficit while prioritising protein, fibre, and realistic habits. |
| Social job | Share meals, wins, and progress for motivation and accountability without being pushed into public comparison. |
| Learning job | Understand why hunger, cravings, energy, and scale fluctuations may change across her cycle. |
