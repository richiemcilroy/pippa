# Distribution And Community Advantage

Source sections: 6.7, 6.8, 10. Founder clarification: Girls in Marketing is behind-the-scenes marketing strategy; share cards launch first; community comes later.

## Girls In Marketing Wedge

The whitepaper says Girls in Marketing gives the product a rare starting advantage:

- An aligned female audience.
- Existing trust.
- Existing social behaviour.
- Shared professional identity.

The app should not simply market to that audience. It should use the audience to shape:

- Product language.
- Share card design.
- Onboarding questions.
- Early community norms.

Founder clarification: this is a behind-the-scenes marketing strategy, not necessarily a visible in-product launch community.

## First Distribution Loop

The first distribution loop should be UGC-led:

1. A user logs a meal, hits a protein/fibre win, or completes a consistent week.
2. The app generates a beautiful card.
3. She shares it to Instagram or TikTok.
4. A friend sees a modern female-focused calorie app that feels more relevant than MyFitnessPal.
5. The friend joins, tracks privately, and later opts into accountability.

Founder clarification: share cards should be available in the first version for sharing to social media, while in-app community comes later. Calories should be hidden by default, protein/fibre/progress should be shown by default, and users should be able to toggle visible metrics.

## Distribution Assets

| Distribution asset | Product source | Why it can work |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Meal card | Photo or logged meal | Women already share food; the app adds useful context without making the post clinical. |
| Protein/fibre card | Targets dashboard | Turns nutrition education into a shareable achievement. |
| Streak card | Consistency engine | Creates identity around showing up. |
| Cycle-aware insight card | Cycle dashboard | Makes the female-focused differentiation visible. |
| Weekly progress card | Weekly summary | Shows progress toward goal without requiring perfect daily tracking. |
| Group challenge card | Community | Turns accountability into social proof while avoiding restriction contests. |

## Community Mechanics

The community should be opt-in. The user starts in a private tracker, then chooses whether to join community, set up a profile, and control visibility.

Founder clarification: community will come later, so these mechanics should be treated as future scope.

Community components:

- Optional community profiles.
- Goal-based, lifestyle-based, or audience-based spaces.
- A natural Girls in Marketing launch group.
- Posts, comments, and likes for support, meal sharing, accountability, and questions.
- Private accountability circles for selected sharing.
- Moderation rules against shame, body checking, extreme restriction, harassment, and medical advice presented as certainty.
