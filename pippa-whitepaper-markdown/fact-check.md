# Fact-Check Pass

Date checked: 2026-05-12.

Scope: selected high-risk claims from the whitepaper, focused on market size/M&A, science, safety/legal framing, and technical stack claims.

Verdict key:

- Supported: claim matches the cited source or a primary/credible source.
- Supported with caveat: claim is directionally supported, but wording should stay cautious.
- Needs follow-up: claim needs deeper legal, clinical, commercial, or technical diligence before being used externally.

## Summary

No checked claim looked obviously invented.

The strongest claims are the product implications that follow from the evidence: fast logging, flexible ranges, cycle-aware context rather than precise calorie changes, privacy by default, and non-shaming design.

The main caution is that external-facing materials should keep legal, medical, and regulatory language conservative. The whitepaper is appropriate for internal strategy, but marketing and onboarding copy should be reviewed separately before launch.

## Recommended Wording Changes

These are wording guardrails, not evidence corrections:

- Keep cycle-calorie language contextual: "may", "likely", "range", "trend", and "starting point".
- Avoid marketing claims that imply medical treatment or precise hormonal prescriptions.
- Treat Reuters M&A/EBITDA/member numbers as reported figures, not audited facts.
- Keep "community" as future scope after founder clarification.
- Avoid UK-first positioning unless a later go-to-market decision makes it explicit.
