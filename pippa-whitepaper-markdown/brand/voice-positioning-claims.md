# Brand Voice, Positioning, And Claims

Source sections: 7, 11. Founder clarification: App Store positioning should include both clear weight-loss language and softer nutrition/consistency language.

## Brand Positioning

The brand should be intentionally female-focused. The whitepaper says that should mean more than pink visuals.

The product should respect the lived experience of women trying to lose weight while balancing:

- Work.
- Social life.
- Hormones.
- Wellness content.
- Self-image.
- Accountability.

Founder clarification: public App Store positioning should say weight loss clearly while also using nutrition and consistency language.

## Voice

The voice should be professional, encouraging, and opinionated.

The whitepaper describes the intended voice as sounding like a smart, calm female nutritionist:

- Direct enough to be useful.
- Warm enough to reduce guilt.
- Restrained enough to avoid pseudoscience.

## Voice Traits

| Voice trait | How it sounds |
| --- | --- |
| Professional | Clear, evidence-aware, not childish, not influencer fluff. |
| Encouraging | Normalises imperfect days and points the user back to the next useful action. |
| Opinionated | Gives practical guidance instead of vague wellness language. |
| Female-aware | Acknowledges cycle context, cravings, bloating, and real-life inconsistency. |
| Non-shaming | Never uses moral language around food or body size. |

## Language System

Use often:

- consistent
- enough
- flexible
- nourishing
- prepared
- progress
- context
- protein
- fibre
- week
- range

Avoid:

- cheat
- bad food
- burn off
- punish
- willpower
- detox
- cleanse
- shred
- bikini body
- guilt-free

## Copy Direction

Use:

- "You are still on track."
- "Higher hunger can be normal this week."
- "Let's anchor your next meal."

Avoid:

- "You failed today."
- "Control your cravings."
- "Make up for it tomorrow."

## Sample UX Copy

Source copy from the whitepaper:

- "You are above target today. That happens. Your week still matters more than one day."
- "You are likely in your luteal phase, so hunger may feel louder. Aim for the higher end of your range and keep protein steady."
- "Protein and fibre are your anchors today. Add one satisfying snack if you need it."
- "The scale can move before your period. Look at the trend, not today's number."
- "You logged four days this week. That is useful data, not a test you passed or failed."

## Responsible Claims

| Responsible claim | Avoid |
| --- | --- |
| "Built for women who want to lose weight sustainably." | "The only calorie tracker women need." |
| "Cycle-aware guidance helps you understand hunger, cravings and progress patterns." | "Your cycle means you need exactly X more calories today." |
| "Track calories, protein, fibre and fat in a way that feels more forgiving." | "Calories do not matter for women." |
| "AI meal logging gives a quick estimate that you can edit." | "AI tracks your food accurately from any photo." |
| "Private by default, social when you choose." | "Share your weight loss with everyone." |
| "This is general wellness support, not medical advice." | "Treat PMS, PCOS, endometriosis or eating disorders." |
