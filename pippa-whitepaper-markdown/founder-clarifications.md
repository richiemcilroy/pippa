# Founder Clarifications

These points come from founder Q&A after the whitepaper conversion. They are not claims from the original whitepaper.

## Confirmed

- Product name: Pippa.
- Domain: `pippa.health`.
- Primary product surface: mobile app.
- Platform sequence: iOS first, then Android.
- UK-first positioning is not required.
- Girls in Marketing is behind-the-scenes marketing strategy, not necessarily a visible launch community inside the product.
- Cycle data should be enterable manually.
- Manual cycle onboarding should start with the basics: last period start date, average cycle length, period length, and regularity.
- Contraception context and irregular-cycle nuance should be introduced later as optional settings, not required in first-run onboarding.
- Future cycle-data connections should likely prioritise Apple Health and Health Connect before assuming direct connectivity to apps such as Flo.
- Community will come later.
- Share cards should be included in the first version because they support social sharing outside the app.
- Share cards should hide calories by default, show protein/fibre/progress by default, and allow users to toggle visible metrics.
- MVP food logging should include AI/photo logging, barcode scanning, saved meals, text/manual entry, and related logging modes from the whitepaper.
- Onboarding should require current weight.
- Onboarding should include a fun goal-direction slider while still allowing an actual goal weight to be entered.
- Authentication should be email OTP only for now.
- Public App Store positioning should include both weight loss and softer nutrition/consistency language.
- The proposed technical stack is mostly decided, but still open to better ideas before implementation.
- A fact-check pass is wanted for selected whitepaper claims.
- Monetisation can be skipped for now.

## Product Implications

- Avoid hard-coding UK-first assumptions into positioning unless they are operationally necessary.
- Treat web as supporting infrastructure or later surface unless separately scoped.
- Keep launch scope focused on iOS-first private mobile tracking, basic manual cycle input, full MVP food logging, targets, insights, and share cards.
- Do not treat community as day-one product scope.
- Treat Apple Health and Health Connect as the first likely cycle-data integration surfaces to investigate.
- Keep Girls in Marketing as an acquisition and audience-learning wedge unless product strategy changes.
- Avoid pricing/subscription decisions until monetisation is explicitly reopened.
